# Úbeda Tourism Guide

Úbeda is a UNESCO World Heritage City in Andalusia, Spain, renowned for its outstanding Renaissance architecture. This guide covers 367 points of interest across the destination.

## Destination Overview

Úbeda is a **UNESCO World Heritage City** (designated in **2003**), located in Andalusia, Spain. It is renowned for its outstanding Renaissance architecture and monumental heritage.

Important and populous city located on the hills of its namesake, in the geographical center of the province, in a landscape populated by olive trees and cereal fields.

Úbeda is an artistic and monumental city, no matter where you look, and one of the most stately and glorious towns in Andalusia. Castilian and Renaissance on all sides, it contains such a quantity of monuments, churches, palaces, and stately homes that the best thing to do is to stroll through it and virtually transport oneself to the time of its historical splendor: The Chapel of El Salvador, located in the incomparable square of the Town Hall, the Church of S. Pablo, the Church of Santa María de los Reales Alcázares, the Church of La Trinidad, the Oratory of San Juan de la Cruz, Monastery of Santa Clara, Vela de los Cobos Palace, Las Cadenas Palace, Casa de las Torres, Santiago Hospital, Hospital of the Honored Old of El Salvador, Clock Tower, Walls of La Cava, Pottery Museum, and the National Parador of Tourism, are some of the wonders that this surprising city contains.

In addition to these attractions, a visit to the walls and the splendid view obtained from there is suggested, as well as to its potters, especially to the house-museum of Paco Titos.

BIC (Cultural Heritage Site) Built around the middle of the 16th century by Andrés de Vandelvira, one of the finest architects of the Spanish Renaissance. Free-standing on all four sides, it is horizontally structured into three floors and vertically into seven differently wide sections, to achieve an optical effect of symmetry and solidity. ### Reproduction of Atlante and Caryatid from the Vázquez de Molina Palace
- **Type**: TouristAttraction
- **Address**: Úbeda, Jaén
- **Country**: Spain
- **Region**: Andalucía
- **Coordinates**: 38.010532, -3.370946
- **Tourism interest**: Architecture
- **Images**: [Image 90127](https://api.inventrip.com/v100/image/90127?image_quality=high), [Image 90128](https://api.inventrip.com/v100/image/90128?image_quality=high)
- **ID**: poi/65811

In Renaissance style, this intervention is a full-scale reproduction of an atlas and a caryatid from the Vázquez de Molina Palace in Úbeda, one of the architectural gems of the city. The initiative was born with the purpose of bringing these unique figures closer to the public, allowing for a better understanding of Úbeda''s heritage and artistic richness.

The original sculptures are located on the top floor of the main facade of the Vázquez de Molina Palace, 16th century, a work by the famous architect Andrés de Vandelvira, one of the great masters of the Andalusian Renaissance, and situated in the square of the same name. In total, the facade houses twelve figures (six atlantes and six caryatids), all different from each other. The atlantes, male representations, and the caryatids, female, are anthropomorphic figures that serve an architectural function by acting as supporting elements. Both figures symbolize the strength, harmony, and nobility characteristic of Renaissance architecture.

The facade of the palace is organized into four levels that combine different architectural orders: Corinthian on the first level, Ionic on the second, and, crowning the ensemble, the atlantes and caryatids bearing the family shields. Particularly striking is the inversion of the usual classical order—Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian—a detail that adds uniqueness to the building. An elegant projecting cornice tops the construction, while two small towers at the corners add volume and depth, completing a facade that invites one to stop and admire each of its details.

### Bonfires of Saint Anthony
- **Map prominence**: Major landmark (zoom 15)
- **Type**: TraditionalFestival
- **Address**: Úbeda, Jaén
- **Country**: Spain
- **Region**: Andalucía
- **Coordinates**: 38.018262, -3.368850
- **Tourism interest**: Party
- **Start date**: 2025-01-16T00:00:00+00:00
- **End date**: 2025-01-16T00:00:00+00:00
- **Images**: [Image 80066](https://api.inventrip.com/v100/image/80066?image_quality=high), [Image 80065](https://api.inventrip.com/v100/image/80065?image_quality=high)
- **ID**: poi/63289

The Festival of the Bonfires of San Antón is a highly anticipated tradition in Úbeda, celebrated in mid-January. During this night, considered the longest of the month and a symbol of the beginning of the coldest months of the year, neighbors and visitors gather around bonfires lit in squares and neighborhoods of the city.

The flames, fueled by olive branches and pruning remains, represent purification and protection for the new year. Next to the fire, typical products such as sardines, blood sausage, or popcorn are enjoyed, while reveling in a festive atmosphere and neighborhood camaraderie. Additionally, the blessing of animals is maintained, a gesture that recalls the agricultural origin of the celebration.

Organized by the City Council with the collaboration of associations and local districts, this night of fire and tradition heralds the winter and strengthens the community bonds of the city.

The San Miguel Fair, from September 28th to October 4th, is one of the most important in the province. According to tradition, it dates back to September 29th, 1233, the feast day of the patron saint, when King Ferdinand III the Saint conquered the city. It is a festival marking the transition from summer to autumn, from the end of the harvest to the beginning of the agricultural year. During the fair, bullfights, a prestigious theater season, numerous concerts, and other cultural events and activities are held both within and outside the fairgrounds.

Coinciding with many of these traditional activities, most of the cultural events are programmed and developed, such as: the Biennial of Antique Dealers, the International Music and Dance Festival "City of Úbeda", the International Film Music Festival "City of Úbeda", the Storytelling Marathon "In Úbeda Stories are Told...", the Youth Week, the Agricultural Machinery Fair, the Tapas Fair, the Autumn Theater Showcase, the Early Music Festival of Úbeda and Baeza, the Commemoration of the death of San Juan de la Cruz, and many other events that take place throughout the year.

### Pottery District and Santo Tomás Route
- **Map prominence**: Major landmark (zoom 16)
- **Type**: Trail
- **Address**: Úbeda, Jaén
- **Country**: Spain
- **Region**: Andalucía
- **Coordinates**: 38.012620, -3.363959
- **Tourism interest**: Architecture, Cultural, Trekking
- **Images**: [Image 89323](https://api.inventrip.com/v100/image/89323?image_quality=high)
- **ID**: poi/65803

In 2012, Úbeda was declared a ''Zone of Artisanal Interest of Andalusia''. The declaration recognizes the relevance and uniqueness of the city''s crafts, as well as the diversity of existing trades and the artistic quality of the pieces, which combine tradition with new designs.

The surviving artisanal trades take us back to a long historical experience: pottery, artistic forging, cabinetmaking, artistic stained glass, vegetable fibers, and Mudejar coffered ceilings are some of the sectors in which the artisans excel, exporting their production to various parts of the national and international geography. Each of their products enjoys the exclusivity and authenticity characteristic of handmade items, resulting from the transformation of noble materials with traditional techniques. Additionally, they have significant recognitions within the artisanal world (Master Craftsmen, National Craftsmanship Award, National Design Award...).

Any item produced in the workshops of Úbeda, whether large or small, simple or complex, is today a museum piece, worthy of being preserved and admired.

### Southern Renaissance Route
- **Type**: Trail
- **Address**: Úbeda, Jaén
- **Country**: Spain
- **Region**: Andalucía
- **Coordinates**: 38.007800, -3.368713
- **Tourism interest**: Architecture, Cultural, Trekking
- **Images**: [Image 89324](https://api.inventrip.com/v100/image/89324?image_quality=high)
- **ID**: poi/65804

This route begins at Plaza Vázquez de Molina. This square constitutes the most important monumental complex of the city and corresponds to the so-called Exceptional Universal Value, EUV, for which UNESCO declared the city of Úbeda a World Heritage Site on July 3, 2003. Made up of a group of civil and religious buildings constructed in just fifty years, it forms the great Renaissance architectural ensemble of Spain and one of the most important in Europe.
